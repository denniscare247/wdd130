Leopard

The Oba is referred to metaphorically as “the leopard of the house,”

and images of the beautiful, cunning, and immensely dangerous cat

appear frequently in Benin's royal arts. Before the British invasion

in 1897, domesticated leopards were kept in the palace to demonstrate

the Oba's mastery over the wilderness. Leopard imagery is also

frequently linked to the Oba's military might
